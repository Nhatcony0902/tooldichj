# Business Email Playbook

For `t1k:human-writing` email register. The user's email is mostly counterparty negotiation, finance/invoicing, and partner comms. Sources verified 200 (end).

## Email-specific AI tells

**Openers** (appear in ~70% of AI-drafted partnership emails):
- "I hope this email finds you well" / "I trust you're doing well"
- "I am reaching out to…" / "I wanted to connect regarding…"
- "In today's fast-paced [industry] landscape…"
- "Dear Sir or Madam"

**Closers** (supportive closers appear ~287% more often in AI email):
- "Please don't hesitate to reach out"
- "Looking forward to hearing from you"
- "Let me know if you have any questions"
- "Feel free to contact me at your earliest convenience"
- "Kindly let me know" ("kindly" is a strong marker)
- "Thank you for your time and consideration"

**Subject lines:** AI writes them title-cased, vague, press-release-style ("Exciting Partnership Opportunity"). Humans write them lowercase, reference-specific, ≤7 words ("Sale partner agreement — next step").

**Register:** over-formal, over-long, no contractions, buzzword-loaded (leverage, synergy, robust, holistic), perfectly polite and completely soulless. The deepest tell is the absence of any point of view.

## The human playbook

1. **One email, one ask.** State it in the subject and again in the first or last line.
2. **Open with the reason, not pleasantries.** "Attached is Invoice #INV-2026-047 — USD 4,800, due 20 June." Not "I hope you're well. I wanted to follow up…"
3. **Use specifics.** Dates, amounts, section numbers, names. "The 50% first-project commission is on gross, not net — worth fixing before we sign" reads human; "I have some questions about the commission" reads AI.
4. **Contractions + short sentences.** If you'd say "we're offering" out loud, don't write "we are offering."
5. **Cut transitions.** Delete "Furthermore / Additionally"; connect directly.
6. **Direct, not aggressive.** "We can't move on the IP clause" beats "after careful consideration we find it difficult to accommodate…"
7. **Ask for one specific small next step.** "Does Thursday 3 PM GMT work?" beats "let me know your availability at your earliest convenience."
8. **Sign off simply.** "Best, Tu" or just "Tu" in a thread.

### Negotiation tone
- Position first, then reasoning.
- Acknowledge briefly, then pivot: "Understood on the IP assignment — our concern is the Phase 1 trigger. Can we tie it to Milestone 2 delivery instead?"
- If you need time, say so: "I need to check with my co-founders. I'll come back Friday." (Silence works in person; in email it just reads as ghosting.)
- No double-hedges: "we can do that" / "we can't do that" both beat "I think it might potentially be possible."

### Finance / invoice tone
- Subject: `Invoice #INV-YYYY-NNN — USD [amount] — due [date]`
- First sentence is the invoice fact, no pleasantry.
- Body ≤3 lines: what it covers, amount + due date, how to pay (bank/SWIFT or link).
- Overdue: one firm-not-angry line — "This invoice is now 7 days overdue — can you confirm when payment will clear?"

## Before / after

**Before (AI — 127 words, zero specifics, 5 flagged phrases):**
> **Subject:** Follow-Up on Contract Discussion — Partnership Opportunity
> Dear Mr. Bach,
> I hope this email finds you well. I am reaching out to follow up on our recent discussion regarding the proposed partnership agreement. I wanted to take this opportunity to reiterate our commitment to fostering a mutually beneficial relationship.
> Kindly let me know if you have had the opportunity to review the terms… We are eager to move forward and would greatly appreciate your feedback at your earliest convenience.
> Please don't hesitate to reach out should you have any questions.
> Thank you for your time and consideration.
> Warm regards and best wishes, Tu

**After (human — 48 words, one fact, one position, one ask):**
> **Subject:** Sale partner agreement — next step
> Bach,
> The draft's been with you since Monday. Main open point is the commission cap in §3 — we're at $3k/project, you proposed dropping it. I can go to $4k but the cap stays.
> Can you confirm by Thursday? We want to close before month-end.
> Tu
